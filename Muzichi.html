Muzichi

2017 - UI Designer

Introduction

I was invited to design a music streaming app with the purpose of exploring friendly and unique interfaces. It was a smaller project and only lasted a couple weeks but it was fun to explore iOS guidelines and work with a team to design cutting edge interfaces. I was given the guidelines and branding for the design and now it was up to me to take that information and design something approachable and unique.

The Sketches

I received the SWOT analyses from the research team on competitors like Spotify, Pandora, and iHeart Radio. These SWOTS helped me understand the competition I was up against and also determine the layout for Muzichi.

Because of the research, I knew I would need to include search, playlist and album and song view features. After going through the research materials I started sketching out the layout.

I knew I needed to come up with an innovative idea for the playing song screen. I wanted it to be engaging. I started sketching out some ideas of what the screen could look like. Out of my different sketches, I chose to go with the interactive bars that would move with the music.

High Fidelity Mockups

Final Thoughts

Working on this project was an excellent experience. It was fun to think through the different things a user would want to accomplish on a music streaming app and then bring those features to life in an approachable way. Music streaming brings so many people daily joy which is why this project was a real win for me as a designer.
